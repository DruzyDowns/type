Early Digital Fonts

With the rise of phototypesetting and the Digiset, fonts had to be designed specifically for the machine. In 1976

The first four commercially available digital typefaces were Marconi & Edison by Hermann Zapf and Demos & Praxis by Gerard Unger.

Univers is a great example of a font developed for phototypesetting that is still in use today.
