A Brief history

Typeface design evolved from the painstaking process of casting metal type.

In the 60's, this process was largely replaced by the rise of the phototypesetter - where complete characters are projected onto photographic material from a negative

In the 70's and 80's, the rise of personal computers gave the average person the ability to write and publish documents without professional help or a typewriter.
